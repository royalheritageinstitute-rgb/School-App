Brand Guidelines: Royal Heritage Learning SPA
1. Brand Identity
Brand Name: Royal Heritage Learning
Mission: To provide a highly engaging, frictionless, and kid-friendly CBSE learning experience for students from Nursery to Class 8.
Vibe/Aesthetic: "Royal Heritage" but modernized for children. Clean, playful, trustworthy, and highly accessible.
2. Color Palette
Primary Colors:
Heritage Blue: #1E3A8A (Deep, trustworthy blue for headers and primary branding).
Royal Gold: #F59E0B (Warm, rewarding gold used for "Stars", achievements, and primary call-to-action buttons).
Secondary/Background Colors:
Soft Pastel Yellow: #FEF3C7 (For Nursery - Class 3 specific zones; friendly and warm).
Neutral Off-White: #F9FAFB (For the main app background to reduce eye strain).
Pure White: #FFFFFF (For content cards and quiz containers).
Success/Error Colors:
Success Green: #10B981 (For correct quiz answers and streak indicators).
Error Red: #EF4444 (For incorrect answers or fallback UI).
3. Typography
Primary Font: Nunito or Quicksand (Google Fonts). These are highly legible, rounded sans-serif fonts excellent for early readers and dyslexia accessibility.
Heading Hierarchy:
H1: Bold, large, playful (used for Week/Module titles).
H2/H3: Semi-bold, clean (used for section dividers).
Body Text: Minimum size 16px (or text-base in Tailwind) to ensure it is easily readable on mobile devices.
4. UI/UX Elements
Buttons: Must be pill-shaped or have heavily rounded corners (rounded-full or rounded-2xl).
Add a slight shadow (shadow-md).
On active/tap, simulate a "bouncy" press effect (active:scale-95).
Cards: Use soft shadows (shadow-sm) and rounded corners (rounded-xl) for video containers, quiz options, and subject modules.
Touch Targets: Absolute minimum of 44x44px for all interactable elements. Give ample padding (p-4) so kids don't accidentally tap the wrong answer.
5. Tone of Voice & Copywriting
General Tone: Encouraging, enthusiastic, simple, and supportive. Use kid-friendly emojis (🚀, ⭐, 🎉, 📚) sparingly but effectively.
Error Messages: Never sound robotic. Instead of "Error 404: Video not found", use "Oops! We couldn't find this video. Let's try another one! 🕵️‍♂️".
6. Strict Bengali Language Rules (Localisation)
As defined in our core system prompts, the app supports English and Bengali. When applying these guidelines to Bengali text:
Rule: You MUST use conversational, easy, and everyday vocabulary (চলতি এবং সহজ ভাষা).
Rule: NO complex, formal, or Sanskritized vocabulary (সাধু ভাষা).
Tone in Bengali: Maintain the same enthusiastic and supportive tone.
Example of encouragement: Use "দারুণ হয়েছে!" (Great job!) instead of "অত্যুৎকৃষ্ট!".
Example of instruction: Use "পরেরটায় চলো" (Let's go to the next one) instead of "পরবর্তী ধাপে অগ্রসর হও".
